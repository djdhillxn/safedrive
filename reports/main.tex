\begin{abstract}
SafeRL-Drive is a reproducible closed-loop autonomous-driving benchmark built with
MetaDrive and Stable-Baselines3. The Phase-1 study trains continuous-control PPO and
SAC agents on procedurally generated roads, selects checkpoints on a fixed validation
range, evaluates frozen policies on a disjoint held-out test range, and compares them
with MetaDrive's rule-based IDM policy. The benchmark records
success, collision, off-road, timeout, route-completion, return, cost, speed, and episode
length metrics while retaining the configurations, logs, models, and plots needed to
reproduce each result. Numerical results in this scaffold are intentionally left as
placeholders until the final Colab experiments are complete.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Closed-loop simulation exposes a driving policy to the consequences of its own actions.
This makes it useful for studying control failures that are hidden by one-step prediction
metrics. SafeRL-Drive focuses on a deliberately small question: how do two standard deep
reinforcement-learning algorithms compare with a rule-based controller under the same
procedural driving evaluation? MetaDrive supplies diverse, configurable scenarios for
this study \cite{li2022metadrive}.

\section{Problem Setup}
The agent receives MetaDrive's vector observation and produces continuous steering and
throttle/brake commands. Training scenarios begin at seed 0. Validation scenarios begin
at seed 1000 and are used only for checkpoint selection. Final evaluation begins at seed
2000 and uses 100 held-out scenarios, with one scenario seed assigned per episode.
Validation and test traffic are fixed; training retains randomized traffic for diversity.

\section{Environment and Agents}
The learning agents are Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) \cite{schulman2017ppo} and
Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) \cite{haarnoja2018sac}, implemented through
Stable-Baselines3 \cite{raffin2021sb3}. PPO uses four vectorized environments; SAC uses
one environment to keep replay-buffer behavior straightforward. MetaDrive's
\texttt{IDMPolicy} is evaluated without training and supplies the rule-based reference.

\section{Experimental Design}
Each learning algorithm is trained once for 500,000 environment timesteps by default.
The training script saves the final model, periodic checkpoints with matching observation
normalization statistics, training diagnostics, and a best validation checkpoint. That
checkpoint is selected first by success rate, then by route completion and lower failure
rates. Only the frozen winner receives the held-out test evaluation used for comparison.
The smoke test verifies installation and wiring but is excluded from the reported results.

\section{Metrics}
The headline metrics are success rate, collision rate, off-road rate, timeout rate, and
mean route completion. Mean return and episode length summarize task performance and
rollout duration. Mean safety cost and speed provide additional context. Success rate is
accompanied by a 95\% Wilson confidence interval. Per-episode CSV files retain scenario
seeds and final simulator information so aggregate values remain auditable.

\section{Discussion}
The final discussion should distinguish task return from driving safety. A controller can
make progress while still colliding or leaving the road, and a conservative policy may
reduce failures at the cost of route completion. The rule-based baseline provides a useful
reference for deciding whether learned behavior is merely active or genuinely competitive.

\section{Limitations and Future Work}
Phase 1 uses one training run per learning algorithm, state-vector observations, one
simulator, and a fixed evaluation budget. Results therefore demonstrate an engineering
benchmark rather than broad statistical superiority. Seed sweeps, richer observations,
and explicit safety mechanisms are deferred until this focused benchmark is complete.

\section{Conclusion}
SafeRL-Drive connects training, held-out scenario evaluation, rule-based comparison,
visualization, video, and artifact logging in one reproducible MetaDrive workflow. The
final report will replace the placeholders with measured PPO, SAC, and IDM results from
the saved Phase-1 comparison files.
